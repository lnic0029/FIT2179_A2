Causes of Death in Australia

Leading Causes of Death in Australia

In 2014, heart diseases were the leading cause of death in New South Wales (NSW), South Australia (SA), and Victoria. In 2015, neoplasms(cancers) took over as the leading cause of death for all regions other than NSW and since 2016, neoplasms have been the leading cause of death for all regions in Australia.

The leading causes of death are all noncommunicable diseases, a mixture of mostly neoplasms (cancers) and heart disease.

The Affect of Age on Causes of Death

Most deaths come from older people, and therefore the leading causes of death are skewed towards the causes of death that most affect elderly people.
