How Causes of Death Differ Between Countries and Over Time

Global Causes of Death

This is an overview of the types of deaths that were most common in each country in 2021. Diseases that are grouped as communicable, maternal, perinatal, and nutritional can be found more commonly in Africa and a few countries in South America, while the majority of countries are led by noncommunicable diseases (e.g. Cancer, Heart Diseases). These categorical causes of death can be more clearly seen for each region below.

Top Categories of Causes of Death Globally

This graph shows the global percentages of causes of death over time, where steady growth can be seen for noncommunicable diseases, up until 2020 and 2021, where the percentage of communicable diseases causing death grows. This can be attributed to the global pandemic caused by COVID-19.

Comparison of Causes of Death Between Countries

This is a comparison of countries by a breakdown of the different causes of death. It shows that the share of noncommunicable and communicable causes of death are inversly proportional, with a few outliers due to a higher proportion of deaths caused by injury.

More developed countries tend to have a higher proportion of noncommunicable deaths as they are more likely to die of causes common to old age such as cancer and heart disease.

Causes of Death in Australia

In 2014, heart diseases were the leading cause of death in New South Wales (NSW), South Australia (SA), and Victoria. In 2015, malignant neoplasms (cancer) took over as the leading cause of death for all regions other than NSW and since 2016, malignant neoplasms have been the leading cause of death for all regions in Australia.

Heart disease was a more common cause of death in 2014 and 2015, but it was already on the decline due to improvements in medical treatments and reduced smoking rates.

Note: Not all neoplasms are cancers, some are benign, but all neoplasms that cause death (malignant neoplasms) are cancers

Top Causes of Death in Australia

The leading causes of death are all noncommunicable diseases, a mixture of mostly malignant neoplasms (cancers) and heart disease, with malignant neoplasms as the leading cause of death of all years by far.

The Affect of Age on Causes of Death

Most deaths come from older people, and therefore the leading causes of death are skewed towards the causes of death that most affect elderly people.
